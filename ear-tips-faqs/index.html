Ear Tips questions, answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The best replacement ear tips for your AirPods or earbuds come down to your ear shape, preferred material, and intended use. Memory foam tips provide the most secure seal and passive noise isolation but wear out faster, while silicone options are durable and easy to clean. For most people, a hybrid approach, trying a few different sizes and materials, is the quickest path to a comfortable, great-sounding fit.

What size ear tips do I need?

Getting the right size is the single most important factor in comfort and sound quality. Most earbuds come with small, medium, and large tips, but these measurements aren’t standardised across brands. A medium in one set may feel like a small in another. The best way to find your size is to insert the tip and gently tug on your earbud, if it feels loose or slips out during movement, you probably need a larger size or a different shape. Keep in mind that your left and right ear canals are rarely identical. It’s common to need a medium in one ear and a large in the other. Many premium aftermarket tips, like the ones from SpinFit or Dekoni, offer multi-size packs so you can mix and match. Don’t be afraid to experiment: a slightly bigger tip than you think you need often provides the best seal and bass response. A good test is to listen to a track with deep bass while you gently move your head. If the bass drops out or sounds thin, the seal has broken and you likely need a different size or material.

Silicone vs. memory foam: which is better?

Silicone tips are the most common because they are durable, easy to clean, and maintain their shape over years of use. They glide in smoothly and are a great choice if you remove and reinsert your earbuds frequently throughout the day. The trade-off is that silicone tends to be less accommodating to oddly shaped ear canals and may not create as airtight a seal as foam. Memory foam tips, popularised by brands like Comply, compress when rolled between your fingers and then expand inside your ear canal. This creates a custom-fit seal that is excellent for noise isolation and bass response. Foam wins on comfort for long listening sessions because it conforms to your ear. The downside: foam collects earwax faster, loses its shape after a few weeks of use, and needs to be replaced more often than silicone. For active use like running or gym workouts, foam tips stay put better, but they can trap sweat and degrade more quickly. Some users prefer silicone for hygiene and longevity, while others swear by foam for the best sound isolation. There is no universal winner, it depends on your priorities.

Do ear tips affect sound quality?

Yes, dramatically. The ear tip is the interface between your earbud and your ear canal, and it determines how much of the sound energy actually reaches your eardrum. A poor seal lets bass frequencies leak out, making music sound thin and tinny. A good seal restores deep, punchy bass and improves clarity across the whole frequency range. Foam tips generally absorb some high-frequency detail (sibilance) while boosting low-end warmth. Silicone tips, especially those with a wider bore (the opening that directs sound), preserve more treble sparkle and detail. Some silicone tips are designed with a double or triple flange to create a deeper seal without compressing the ear canal too much. If you find that your earbuds sound harsh or shrill, switching to a foam tip might soften the treble. If they sound muddy or muffled, a silicone tip with a wider bore could bring back clarity. The same earbud can sound radically different with different tips, it’s one of the cheapest and most effective upgrades you can make.

How do I clean and maintain ear tips?

Silicone tips can be removed and washed with mild soap and warm water. Let them air dry completely before reattaching them. Avoid using alcohol or harsh cleaners, as they can degrade the silicone over time. A quick wipe with a damp cloth after each use helps prevent buildup of earwax and bacteria. Foam tips should not be washed with water; moisture destroys their memory-foam structure. Instead, clean them gently with a dry cloth or a specialised cleaning putty that picks up wax without soaking the foam. Most foam manufacturers recommend replacing them every two to four weeks, depending on how often you use them. For any tip, regularly inspect the mesh grille on the earbud nozzle. A clogged mesh is often mistaken for a dying battery or a bad tip. If you notice a sudden drop in volume or a distorted sound, clean the mesh with a dry, soft-bristled brush (some brands include a cleaning tool) before blaming the tips.

Are universal ear tips compatible with my AirPods?

Most aftermarket ear tips are designed to fit a range of earbuds with standard nozzle sizes, but compatibility isn’t guaranteed. Apple’s AirPods Pro and AirPods Pro 2 use a proprietary oval-shaped nozzle that requires tips made specifically for them. Standard round tips will not fit securely. However, many third-party brands now produce tips tailored to the AirPods Pro’s oval housing, look for models that explicitly state compatibility. For standard AirPods that do not have replaceable tips (like the original AirPods), third-party silicone sleeves that slip over the whole earpiece can improve grip and comfort, but they don’t create a deep ear-canal seal. Those designs are more about preventing the earbuds from falling out than improving sound quality. For most other true wireless earbuds (Samsung Galaxy Buds, Sony WF series, Jabra, etc.), the nozzle is round and typically 4–6 mm in diameter. Many universal tips are designed to fit multiple sizes by bending or using a core adapter. When in doubt, check the manufacturer’s website for a compatibility list or look for owner feedback on forums like Reddit.

How often should I replace my ear tips?

There is no strict expiration date, but you’ll know it’s time when the tip no longer stays in place, looks deformed, or feels less grippy. Silicone tips can last a year or more with regular cleaning, but they can harden or tear over time, especially if exposed to heat or direct sunlight. If they start to feel stiff or lose their transparency, replace them. Foam tips have a much shorter lifespan because the material slowly loses its ability to expand and spring back. Most foam manufacturers suggest a new pair every two to four weeks with daily use. When you notice that the foam takes longer to expand, or it doesn’t return to its original shape after being compressed, it’s gone. Eyeball your tips once a month. Look for cracks, tears, or any sign of wear. A torn tip can leave a piece inside your ear canal or affect sound drastically. Replacing tips is inexpensive and can restore the performance of your earbuds almost instantly.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use third-party ear tips with AirPods Pro?

Yes, but you need tips specifically designed for the AirPods Pro’s oval nozzle. Many reputable brands like Comply, SpinFit, and Dekoni make compatible versions. Standard round tips will not stay on securely.

Will ear tips affect the active noise cancellation?

Absolutely. ANC works best when there is a tight acoustic seal around your ear. If your tips let outside noise in, the ANC circuitry has to work harder and may still not cancel low frequencies effectively. A good seal improves both passive isolation (from the tips themselves) and the performance of ANC.

How do I measure my ear canal for the right tip size?

There is no precise measurement you can do at home because ear canals are flexible and not perfectly round. Instead, use the trial-and-fit method: start with the medium tips that came with your earbuds, then go up or down one size until you get a comfortable seal that doesn’t hurt after an hour of wear. Some brands include a sizing guide card, but most people find their size through simple testing.

Are memory foam tips better for sleeping?

Foam tips are generally more comfortable for side sleeping because they squish rather than push against the ear canal. However, they can also trap more pressure if your ear is pressed against a pillow for hours. Some users prefer low-profile silicone tips for sleep. It’s personal, but foam is a popular choice.

Do ear tips affect microphone quality?

Indirectly, yes. A poorly fitting tip can cause the earbud to shift or fall out, which may move the microphone away from your mouth or cause handling noise. A secure fit keeps the earbud stable, so your voice sounds more consistent during calls. However, the tip itself does not filter or redirect sound to the mic.

Can I boil silicone tips to sterilize them?

It is not recommended. Boiling can warp the shape and degrade the silicone, making the tips lose their snug fit. A thorough wash with warm soapy water is sufficient to clean them. If you need to kill bacteria, a very mild bleach solution (followed by rinsing) is safer than boiling, but plain soap and water works well for daily hygiene.

In shortThe perfect ear tip is the one you forget you’re wearing. Don’t settle for the stock pair if they cause discomfort or let sound leak out. Trying a few different materials and sizes is a low-cost experiment that can transform your listening experience. Whether you prefer the plush grip of memory foam or the clean durability of silicone, the upgrade is usually worth the small investment.
