How to choose a ear tips

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

Choose replacement ear tips by focusing on material (foam for grip and passive noise isolation, silicone for durability and easy cleaning), size (the most important factor for comfort and seal), and compatibility with your earbud nozzle diameter. Start with the size that feels snug but not painful, then experiment with materials based on your listening environment and ear shape.

What material should your ear tips be?

The two main materials are foam and silicone, with hybrids appearing more often. Foam tips compress as you insert them, then expand to fill your ear canal. They provide a very secure fit and excellent passive noise isolation, which makes them popular for commuting or noisy environments. The trade-off is that foam wears down faster, can absorb sweat and earwax, and needs more frequent replacement. Well-regarded examples include Comply foam tips, which come in different models for various nozzle shapes. Silicone tips are the standard on most earbuds out of the box. They are non-porous, easy to clean with a quick wipe, and last much longer than foam. Silicone tips often come in multiple bore shapes, narrow, wide, or dual-flange, which affect sound and comfort. Brands like SpinFit and Final Audio make silicone tips with special swivel mechanisms or unusual bore shapes to improve fit and sound. Silicone is generally better for sports because it resists moisture and stays grippy even when wet.

How to find the right size for your ears

Size is the single most important factor. A poor seal means you lose bass, let in outside noise, and risk the earbud falling out. Most earbuds ship with three silicone sizes (small, medium, large), but aftermarket tips often offer extra sizing like extra-small or extra-large, plus in-between lengths. Start with the size that feels snug when you insert the earbud and twist it slightly to seat it. You can test the seal by playing music and gently pushing the earbud deeper, if the bass gets noticeably fuller, your current size may be too small. Many ear canals are different sizes between left and right, so do not hesitate to use different tips in each ear. Multi-size sample packs are an affordable way to explore without committing to one size.

Do ear tips change the sound of your earbuds?

Yes, ear tips can noticeably alter the frequency response of your earbuds. Foam tips tend to absorb some high-frequency energy, which can roll off treble slightly, but they improve bass response dramatically when a good seal is achieved. The result is a warmer, fuller sound that many people prefer for casual listening. Silicone tips with a wide bore (a larger opening at the end) preserve more treble detail and give a more open soundstage. Narrow-bore silicone tips can boost bass by channeling more pressure to the eardrum, but may reduce clarity in the upper mids. Dual-flange or triple-flange silicone tips provide deep insertion and strong isolation, but the sound signature becomes more intimate. Try a couple of tip materials and bore styles to find what matches your taste.

Which ear tips are compatible with my earbuds?

Compatibility depends on the nozzle, the tube that holds the ear tip. Most earbuds use a round nozzle with an outer diameter of about 4.5 to 5.5 millimeters, but some are larger or smaller. You can measure your nozzle with a ruler or check the product page for the nozzle size. Many aftermarket tips list compatible diameters. There is a special case for the AirPods Pro, which use an oval-shaped nozzle. Not all round tips will stay on securely. Several companies, including Comply and SpinFit, make tips specifically shaped to fit the AirPods Pro connector. For standard AirPods (non-Pro), replacement tips are not an option because the earbuds have a hard shell without a removable tip. Always check whether your earbud model has a detachable silicone tip before buying replacements.

How often should you replace ear tips?

The lifespan depends heavily on the material and how you use them. Foam tips degrade relatively quickly: they lose their memory and become less springy after several weeks of daily use. If a foam tip no longer expands fully after you roll it, it is time to replace it. Most foam tip manufacturers suggest changing them every few months. Silicone tips last much longer, often a year or more, because the material does not compress permanently. However, silicone can harden over time or become sticky with accumulated earwax. Signs that silicone tips need replacing include visible cracks, a shiny glazed surface that no longer grips, or a tendency for the tip to slide off the nozzle. Regular cleaning with mild soap and water extends their life significantly.

Silicone vs. foam ear tips for exercise and work

For workouts, foam tips have an advantage in grip: they expand against the ear canal walls and stay put even when you sweat. But foam absorbs moisture, and if you do not dry them thoroughly, they can become a breeding ground for bacteria. Some foam tips are coated with a water-resistant layer, but they are still less hygienic than silicone in the long run. Silicone tips are more resistant to moisture and easier to clean after a gym session. The downside is that sweat can make the surface slippery, especially with cheaper tips. For a work environment, foam tips provide excellent noise isolation for focusing in open offices, while silicone tips allow more situational awareness if you need to hear coworkers. Many people keep one set of each material and swap depending on the activity.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use ear tips made for other brands on my AirPods?

Only if your AirPods are the Pro model (first or second generation) which have removable silicone tips. The standard AirPods have a hard plastic shell and cannot accept replacement tips. For AirPods Pro, you need tips designed specifically for the oval-shaped nozzle; many brands like Comply and SpinFit offer dedicated AirPods Pro versions.

Do memory foam tips last longer than silicone?

No, memory foam tips have a shorter lifespan because the foam material slowly loses its ability to expand after repeated compression. Silicone tips are more durable and can last many months or even years with proper care. Foam tips typically need replacing every few months depending on usage.

How do I clean my ear tips properly?

Remove the tips from the earbuds. For silicone, wash them with mild soap and warm water, then rinse and air dry completely. For foam, do not submerge them fully, instead, gently wipe the surface with a damp cloth and mild soap, then squeeze out excess moisture and let them air dry. Avoid alcohol or harsh chemicals on foam tips as they can break down the material.

Will bigger ear tips always give better noise isolation?

No. Noise isolation depends on a good seal, not on absolute size. A tip that is too large for your ear canal will leak sound and may be uncomfortable, while a properly fitted medium can provide excellent isolation. Start with a size that feels snug and comfortable, then test the seal by talking or shaking your head, if the earbud stays in place and you do not hear a sudden drop in bass, the size is likely correct.

Can ear tips fall off inside my ear canal?

It is very rare with well-designed tips that fit snugly on the nozzle, but it can happen with cheap or poorly fitting tips. To avoid this, make sure the core of the ear tip grips the nozzle securely, you should hear or feel a click when it snaps into place. If you pull on the earbud and the tip stays behind, that means the tip is too loose on the nozzle. Replace it with a tighter model or one that has a locking ring (like some Comply tips for AirPods Pro).

Are foam or silicone ear tips better for sleeping?

Foam tips are generally preferred for sleeping because they are softer and compress to match the shape of your ear canal, reducing pressure points when you lie on your side. Silicone tips can create a firmer, more noticeable pressure against the ear canal wall. However, foam tips may trap more earwax and need more frequent cleaning if you wear them every night.

In shortThe right ear tips can transform your listening experience by delivering better comfort, fit, and sound quality. Start with size, then explore materials based on your priorities. Replace them when they show wear, and always clean them regularly. A small investment in good ear tips is one of the cheapest upgrades you can make to your earbuds.
